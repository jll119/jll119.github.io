Inverse Kinematic Controlled Robotic Arm - March 2022

The inverse kinematic controlled robotic arm is developed as part of Year 3 Robotic Manipulation module at Imperial College London.

Various knowledge from different disciplines such as Maths and Physics were required to build the controller. Several kinematic equations were used to determine the motion of the robot to the desired motion.

Throughout the project, MATLAB was also used constantly to model the movements of the robotic arm insert before testing it on the actual system. Furthermore, 3D designs using CAD was also carried out to design tools needed to complete tasks such as grippers for the robotic arm and pen holders to enable the robotic arm to pick up a pen. The modules were then 3D printed.
